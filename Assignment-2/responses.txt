## Responses

-Data can be sent from one device to another over the internet through
a package. Packets send binary information and often large files have to be
broken into several packets in order to be sent efficiently.
-An IP or internet protocol address is a series of numbers that each specific device
has in order to be identified. A DNS associates name with an IP address, making it easier
to identify.
-A cookie is basically an ID card that a website sends to your computer after
you have visited the website. The purpose of a cookie it to give your computer
a piece of information that can be called when you re-visit that site in order to
be recognized.
-The three main parts to a URL are protocol, domain name and path.
